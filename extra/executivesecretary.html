Executive Secretary: Ololade Ojomo

Ololade Ojomo is a pastor of Victory House Houston, he is also a community leader in the Nigerian diaspora in Houston. A serial entrepreneur he has vast knowledge in startups and development of business alliances. He started out as an exporter of commercial quantities of hardwood to Europe and also cash crops like Shea nut and Cocoa. He formed Allied Development Resources and Codes & Quanta, Nigeria which was a major building contractor in Nigeria and one of five major contractors that built the Oba Erekesan Market (9mn USD project) in Akure, Ondo State. He later partnered with Cartil Construzioni Nigeria an Italian Construction firm which had amongst its projects the construction of 336 Homes (14mn USD project) for The Nigerian Army amongst several road and construction projects. On behalf of IHS Towers, Allied development resources was also involved in the installation of over 25 base stations in Nigeria.

Ten years ago, his wife and life partner got job in the USA and the family decided to relocate to America. In the last 10 years he has focused on building the church in Houston which now owns its building as well as serves as a consultant to Oil & Gas companies in Nigeria mainly in the areas of procurement. His most recent supply was carried out in conjunction with 2545 South LLC for the supply of over 50,000mt of pipes and materials for Mobil Producing Nigeria ‘s Osok Revamp Platform project. Ololade Ojomo was at the helm of the committee that coordinated the visit of the Nigerian Vice President Prof Yemi Osibajo to Houston in 2016 and was also project manager for the Nigerian Initiative for Economic Development Group which took a team of over twenty American businesses to meet with the Nigerian Government in 2017.

He played a significant role in the recent re-election campaign of Mayor Sylvester Turner and served as a liaison between him and the African faith community. He was instrumental in planning a major breakfast meeting during the re-election campaign with over 200 African pastors in attendance. As a member of the fundraising team of African Missions (AMNA) (a nonprofit unit of the Redeemed Christian Church) that raises money to give back to the less privileged in Africa building bore holes, vocational centers and so much more.

Pastor Ololade played an integral role in developing a mobile application project that networked the Redeemed Christian Church of God Churches (800 in North America). The project called AMNA APP was implemented successfully. The application was designed to offer Push notifications, Newsletter section, Mobile Shop section, Event Calendar section which eventually led to donations of over $150k to the furtherance of the African Missions objectives.
